Solving the Ecosystem Game In-Person

Why can't I just solve it algorithmically?

The ecosystem game is a constraint satisfaction problem combining:

  • Interval intersection ‚Äî all species must share overlapping habitat ranges
  • Graph reachability ‚Äî every consumer needs a food source in your selection
  • Network flow ‚Äî calories provided must exceed calories consumed
  • Combinatorial selection ‚Äî choosing 8 from 39 cards

There's no matrix transformation or closed-form solution. The selection problem is NP-hard (finding an 8-clique in a compatibility graph). Even simplified, you'd need to:

  • Track a 39√ó39 compatibility matrix (1,521 cells)
  • Check food chain validity via adjacency matrix powers
  • Solve a linear program for caloric flow

None of this is feasible by hand in 60 minutes. The game is designed for structured elimination, not algebraic solution.

Step-by-Step Method

1
Scan & Identify Producers (3-5 min)

Find the 2-3 producers (calories needed = 0) with the widest environmental ranges and highest calories provided. These anchor your ecosystem.

2
Pick Anchor & Set Habitat Window (2 min)

Choose your primary producer. Write down its ranges ‚Äî this is your initial habitat window. All future picks must fit within it.

3
Add Species Bottom-Up (15-20 min)

For each new species, ask three questions in order:

  1. Does it fit the habitat window? (If no ‚Üí skip)
  2. Can it eat something already selected? (If no ‚Üí skip)
  3. Does it narrow the window dangerously? (Update bounds)
4
Calorie Check (3-5 min)

Sum up: Total Provided (all species) vs Total Needed (consumers only). Provided should be ‚â• 1.5√ó needed for safety margin.

5
Verify & Select Habitat (5 min)

Confirm every consumer can point to its food source. Pick habitat values in the middle of your final window ranges.

Algorithm Details

How does the solver work?

The solver uses constraint propagation with backtracking:

  1. Precompute compatibility ‚Äî Build a graph where edges connect species with overlapping habitat ranges
  2. Start with producers ‚Äî They have no caloric needs and anchor the food chain
  3. Greedy selection with pruning ‚Äî Add species that fit the current habitat window and have valid food sources
  4. Caloric validation ‚Äî Check that total provided ‚â• total needed
  5. Backtrack on failure ‚Äî If stuck, undo last choice and try alternatives

For 39 species, this typically finds solutions in under a second. The search space is pruned aggressively by habitat compatibility.
