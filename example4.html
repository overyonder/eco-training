Example 4 Mountain Hard

Problem Statement

Select 8 species from the 15 available below. This is a challenging Red Rock/Mountain problem with tight elevation constraints and competitive apex predators.

Challenge

The producers have non-overlapping elevation ranges. You must choose which elevation band to target, then build a food chain that works within that narrow window. Multiple predators compete for limited prey.

Hint: Check elevation overlaps FIRST. Some producer combinations are impossible. The Wolf and Mountain Lion cannot both survive — pick one or neither.

Critical Constraints

Elevation Conflict: Tundra Moss (2500–3500m) doesn't overlap with Spruce Forest (1200–2200m). You cannot use both producers.
Wolf vs Mountain Lion: Wolf needs 1100 cal, Mountain Lion needs 1000. Their prey overlaps. If Wolf eats first (1300 Provided > 1200), it may consume Elk (1100) leaving Mountain Lion without enough food.
High-altitude chain: If you choose Tundra Moss zone (2500+ m), your only herbivores are Lemming, Pika, Mountain Goat, and Ptarmigan. That's only 4 consumers — you need 5 more species but options are limited.

Reveal Solution

Valid 8-Species Selection

Alpine Sedge, Mountain Willow, Pika, Bighorn Sheep, Marmot, Elk, Red Fox, Golden Eagle

Habitat Window

Elevation: 2200–2400m | Temp: 2–8°C

Place at: Elevation 2300m, Temp 5°C

Why This Selection?

  • Producers: Alpine Sedge (2200–3000m) + Mountain Willow (1800–2600m) = overlap at 2200–2600m
  • Skip apex predators: Wolf (1100 needed) and Mountain Lion (1000 needed) are too hungry
  • Mid-tier predators: Red Fox (550) and Golden Eagle (650) can be sustained
  • Herbivore buffer: Elk (1100 Provided) gives large prey base

Key Insight

This problem requires targeting the mid-elevation band (2200–2600m) where Alpine Sedge and Mountain Willow overlap. The apex predators (Wolf, Mountain Lion) are traps — their calorie demands exceed sustainable prey. Even Golden Eagle is borderline. The cleanest solution uses only Red Fox as the top predator.
